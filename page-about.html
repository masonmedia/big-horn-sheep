A Band.

BIGHORNSHEEP

Big Horn Sheep blend elements of funk, jazz, electronica, rock, and contemporary improvisation. The Toronto based guitar-bass-drums instrumental trio draws on influences from Rudder to Pomo, Wayne Krantz, Nerve, Tycho, and Boards of Canada. Their all-original repertoire integrates diverse stylistic interests resulting in a unique sound with lyrical melodies, electronic textures, danceable beats, and fluid improvisation. The band recently released a 4-track EP, "EPisode1", and is in preproduction for a full length album.

MASON

Big Horn Sheep bassist and composer Andrew Mason has performed & recorded with a range of Canadian artists including Juno nominated vocalist Aline Morales, Gord Sheard, Al Webster, Luanda Jones, Stacey Kaniuk, Paige Armstrong, Batucada Carioca, Baque de Bamba, Samba Squad, and more. A prolific composer and bandleader, he has written for and recorded with 5 of his own original ensembles (including Big Horn Sheep), writes regularly for his music blog Beats and Interference, and teaches guitar and bass for his own Mason Music Company.

WHITFORD

Neil Whitford is a prolific guitarist, composer, songwriter, and producer. His writing and recording credits include 4 albums with the Ninja Funk Orchestra, 3 with the Circles Quartet, and additional albums with Chloe Charles, Sacred Balance, Mackenzie Longpre Group and Najuah. He has been a musical director for Chloe Charles, Candice Sand, and Alison Jane, with production credits including tracks for Chloe Charles, Alison Jane, Will Conquer, and Georgian Bay. He has been a talent consultant for SonaBLAST! Records Canada, was the director of Toronto Music Camp for 5 years, and is the owner of Oakwood Guitar School.

CLAXTON

Big Horn Sheep drummer Robin Claxton has an extensive list of performing and recording credits, including iconic jazz figures Dame Cleo Laine, the late Sir John Dankworth, Matt Dusk, Guido Basso, Lorne Lofsky, the late Peter Appleyard, Maria Schneider, and Jackie Richardson. He has recorded on several albums and toured extensively across Canada, including the Vancouver 2010 Winter Olympics with saxophonist Taylor Cook, as well as Nevada and California with singer-song writer JP Saxe and Anthony Hall. Claxton also has credits performing musical theatre, most recently with Drayton Entertainment's production of Snow White.
